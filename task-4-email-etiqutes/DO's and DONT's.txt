Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

As a Student Digital Ambassador, use this checklist to ensure your online presence remains professional and responsible.

✅ The Do's (Best Practices)

Do use privacy settings: Regularly review and update your privacy settings to control who can see your posts and personal information.

Do think before you post: Ask yourself if you would be comfortable with a future employer, professor, or parent seeing your content.

Do fact-check information: Verify the authenticity of news or articles before sharing them to prevent the spread of misinformation.

Do network professionally: Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni and industry professionals in your field of study.

Do credit original creators: When sharing someone else's work, art, or ideas, always provide proper attribution or tags.

❌ The Don'ts (Avoid These)

Don't engage in cyberbullying: Never post hurtful, harassing, or exclusionary comments about peers, faculty, or anyone else.

Don't share sensitive data: Avoid posting photos of your college ID, admit cards, hostel room numbers, or private contact details.

Don't post during class/lectures: Respect the academic environment; social media usage during class can be seen as a lack of discipline.

Don't use unprofessional language: Avoid excessive slang, profanity, or aggressive tone, as your digital footprint is permanent.

Don't "overshare" emotions: Avoid posting heat-of-the-moment rants about professors or the university; address grievances through the proper official channels instead.
